## The Structural Precedents

**The Oracle at Dodona (~2400 BCE)** — the oldest documented sound-oracle. Priestesses listened to bronze cauldrons struck by wind-chains and sacred oak foliage to receive prophecy. Different acoustic phenomena (rustling, ringing, rumbling) mapped to different message types. VIBE Reading is this: an acoustic oracle that listens to the present moment (moon, tide, weather) and returns a sound prescription.

**Nada Brahma** — "the universe is sound." Vedic teaching (~800–200 BCE). VIBE operates at *Vaikhari* (audible, produced sound). The six intent states are a progression toward *Pashyanti* (subconscious, pre-verbal sound). Floating is the closest to Pashyanti. The app is not producing sound — it is revealing it.

**Wǔ Yīn (五音)** — the Taoist/TCM five-tone pentatonic system, the oldest surviving tonal-healing system. The 8 I Ching trigrams map to 5 elements, and each element to a pentatonic note: Earth/Mountain=C(512Hz), Metal/Heaven=D(576Hz), Wood/Wind/Thunder=E(640Hz), Fire=G(768Hz), Water=A(864Hz). Ancient ritual used 64 bronze bells — one per hexagram — tuned to these notes. The I Ching is a tonal system. VIBE's elemental section is its contemporary form.

**136.1 Hz** — the "OM frequency," derived from Earth's orbital year reduced to audible Hz (Cousto method). This is the heart chakra anchor in multiple traditions and the foundation frequency of VIBE's deep meditation and floating presets. It is also close to C# in just intonation tuning, placing it in the Dorian mode of the Wǔ Yīn system.

**The Ombak Pulse** — Balinese gamelan deliberately tunes paired instruments 3–5 Hz apart, producing a slow amplitude beating called *ombak* ("wave"). This beating falls in the theta-to-delta range (the frequency of deep meditation and dreaming). VIBE's noise channels carry an LFO-modulated pulse at the binaural beat target for the current reading — amplitude modulation instead of pitch beating, but the same phenomenon.

## The Physics

Sound combinations that consistently feel pleasant or mystic follow three laws:
1. **Ratio law**: frequency pairs at 3:2 (fifth), 4:3 (fourth), 5:4 (major third), 2:1 (octave) — simple integer ratios minimize roughness and maximize resonance
2. **1/f law**: noise should have 1/f spectral shape (pink noise) — this matches the temporal structure of the brain's own neural activity and all natural ambient sounds
3. **Entrainment law**: slow amplitude modulation (2–14 Hz) in the carrier sound entrains brainwaves toward that frequency band — delta for floating, theta for meditating, alpha for calming
